IQDUBBING-VC: PROSODY MODELING BASED ON DISCRETE SELF-SUPERVISED SPEECH REPRESENTATION FOR EXPRESSIVE VOICE CONVERSION

Abstract: Voice conversion (VC) can successfully convert timbre. However, modeling the expressive speech prosody is a challenge, such as dubbing. In this study, based on recognition-synthesis framework, a new prosody modeling method based on implicit feature is proposed. Firstly, Discrete Self-supervised Speech Representation (DSSR), specifically VQ-Wav2Vec (VQ), is used as implicit feature for prosody modeling to get prosody vector. However, it is found that the prosody vector is mixed with content information which should be sieved. And then, Random Downsample Prosody Sieve (RDPS) is adopted to sieve prosody vector. But it leads to much loss of prosody and is sensitive to random interference. Finally, we propose a Phone Level Prosody Sieve (PLPS). Experiments show that our approach is superior to baseline and comparison systems in terms of sound quality while maintaining prosody and similarity.

System Description

CS: comparison system, which is similar to [12]. It is based on BL, and Mel is used as prosody feature.

BL: baseline system, which refers to Tacotron2 framework, composed of encoder and autoregressive decoder. Only EV is the input of the system.

P1: proposed system 1, in which Mel is replaced with VQ in CS

P2: proposed system 2, in which RDPS is added to prosody vector in P1.

P3(proposed): proposed system 3, in which RDPS is added to prosody vector in P1
